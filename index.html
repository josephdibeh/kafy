01 · Product direction

The opportunity is bigger than a visual refresh.

The current application is visually structured around marketplace browsing and adding products to a cart. A procurement buyer operates with a more focused, repetitive and operational mental model.

What it communicates today

A promotional marketplace where nearly every element competes for equal attention and the home screen behaves like a storefront.

What it should communicate

A professional procurement environment that helps buyers recognize priorities, repeat frequent actions and complete work with confidence.

02 · Procurement mental model

A buyer is not casually browsing for milk.

Restaurants and cafés usually enter the product with a specific operational need. The redesigned experience should reflect that reality.

Frequent behavior

Replenishing frequently purchased stock

The product should make recurring purchases and known requirements faster to access.

Decision behavior

Comparing suppliers for a known requirement

Price, availability, reliability and previous experience matter more than promotional content.

Efficiency behavior

Repeating a previous order

The interface should recognize that speed often comes from repetition, not discovery.

Operational behavior

Tracking what happened after checkout

The value of procurement continues through order status, receiving, documents, exceptions and follow-up.

03 · Value after delivery
